## 🔴 CRITICAL Findings (4)

### F001: Incorrect height threshold for construction fall protection

**Document Section:** Fall Protection Requirements - Construction Industry (1926)

**Issue:** Document states construction fall protection is required at 6 feet, but then incorrectly states scaffolds require protection at 10 feet. OSHA requires fall protection at 6 feet for all construction activities including scaffolds.

**Document States:**
> Scaffolds used during construction type activity requires fall protection to be used at 10 feet or more above a lower level.

**OSHA Requirement (1926.501(b)(1)):**
> Each employee on a walking/working surface with an unprotected side or edge which is 6 feet or more above a lower level shall be protected

**Recommended Action:** Remove the 10-foot scaffold exception and require fall protection at 6 feet for all construction activities including scaffolds

### F002: Incorrect guardrail height specification

**Document Section:** Fall Protection Systems - Guardrail Systems

**Issue:** Document states top rail is 42 inches +/- 3 inches but fails to specify the acceptable range of 39 to 45 inches clearly, and states toe boards must be 4 inches when OSHA requires 3.5 inches minimum.

**Document States:**
> Toe boards must be four inches in height

**OSHA Requirement (1926.502(j)):**
> Toeboards shall be at least 3.5 inches tall from the top edge of the toeboard to the walking/working surface

**Recommended Action:** Change toeboard height requirement to 'at least 3.5 inches' to match OSHA specification

### F003: Prohibited body belt language present

**Document Section:** Fall Protection Systems - Personal Fall Arrest Systems

**Issue:** Document states 'Full body harnesses are required. The use of body belts is prohibited.' but earlier mentions body harnesses with lanyards for aerial lifts without clearly prohibiting body belts for fall arrest in all contexts.

**Document States:**
> Body harnesses must be worn with a lanyard, not to exceed 3 feet in length

**OSHA Requirement (1926.502(d)(18)):**
> Body belts are not acceptable as part of a personal fall arrest system. Body belts may only be used in positioning device systems

**Recommended Action:** Clarify that body belts are prohibited for fall arrest systems and may only be used for positioning device systems, and specify full body harness requirement for all fall arrest applications

### F004: Missing anchorage strength specification per employee

**Document Section:** Fall Protection Systems - Personal Fall Arrest Systems - Anchorage

**Issue:** Document states anchorage must support 5000 lb static load but fails to specify this is per employee attached, which is a critical safety requirement.

**Document States:**
> Structure must be sound and capable of withstanding a 5000 lb. static load.

**OSHA Requirement (1926.502(d)(15)):**
> Anchorages used for attachment of personal fall arrest equipment shall be capable of supporting at least 5,000 pounds per employee attached

**Recommended Action:** Specify that anchorage must support 5,000 pounds per employee attached to ensure adequate capacity for multiple users

### F005: Missing rescue plan requirement

**Document Section:** Fall Protection Systems - Personal Fall Arrest Systems

**Issue:** Document mentions rescue must be 'immediately available' but does not specify the employer's duty to provide prompt rescue or ensure self-rescue capability as required by OSHA.

**Document States:**

**OSHA Requirement (1926.502(d)(17)):**

**Recommended Action:** Add specific requirement that employer must provide for prompt rescue of employees or ensure self-rescue capability, and develop written rescue procedures

### F006: Missing post-impact inspection and removal requirement

**Document Section:** Fall Protection Systems - Personal Fall Arrest Systems

**Issue:** Document states equipment subjected to impact load shall be destroyed but does not address the requirement for inspection and potential reuse after professional evaluation.

**Document States:**
> Harnesses or lanyards that have been subjected to an impact load shall be destroyed.

**OSHA Requirement (1926.502(d)(16)):**
> Personal fall arrest systems and components subjected to impact loading shall be immediately removed from service and not used again until inspected and determined to be undamaged and suitable for reuse

**Recommended Action:** Revise to require immediate removal from service and professional inspection before determining if equipment should be destroyed or can be returned to service
