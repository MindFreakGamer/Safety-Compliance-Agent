## 🔴 CRITICAL Findings (3)

### F001: Incorrect Fall Protection Trigger Height

**Document Section:** Policy & Procedure section

**Issue:** Document specifies 4-foot trigger height for fall protection instead of OSHA's mandatory 6-foot requirement. This is a direct violation of multiple OSHA sections requiring protection at 6 feet or more.

**Document States:**
> fall protection is required anytime an employee/student is exposed to a four foot fall hazard

**OSHA Requirement (1926.501(b)(1)):**
> Each employee on a walking/working surface with an unprotected side or edge which is 6 feet or more above a lower level shall be protected

**Recommended Action:** Change all references from 4 feet to 6 feet throughout the document to match OSHA requirements

### F002: Wrong OSHA Standard Referenced

**Document Section:** Goals section

**Issue:** Document references OSHA 1910 Subpart D for construction work, when construction activities must comply with 1926 Subpart M Fall Protection standards.

**Document States:**
> comply with OSHA Subpart D – Walking-Working Surfaces

**OSHA Requirement (1926.500):**
> Subpart M applies to all construction workplaces where workers are exposed to fall hazards

**Recommended Action:** Replace all references to 1910 Subpart D with 1926 Subpart M for construction activities

### F003: Incorrect Guardrail Height Specification

**Document Section:** Guardrails section

**Issue:** Document specifies guardrail height as 42 inches without the required tolerance range, potentially allowing non-compliant installations.

**Document States:**
> Guardrail systems should contain a 42" high top-rail

**OSHA Requirement (1926.502(b)(1)):**
> Top edge of guardrail systems shall be 42 inches plus or minus 3 inches above the walking/working surface

**Recommended Action:** Specify guardrail height as 42 inches ± 3 inches (39-45 inches acceptable range)

### F004: Incorrect Anchorage Strength for Restraint Systems

**Issue:** Document specifies 1000 lb anchor for restraint systems, but OSHA requires 5000 pounds for all personal fall arrest equipment anchorages.

**Document States:**

**OSHA Requirement (1926.502(d)(15)):**

**Recommended Action:** Change restraint system anchorage requirement to 5,000 pounds per person to match OSHA standards

**Document States:**

**Recommended Action:** Expand training curriculum to include all eight required OSHA training topics
