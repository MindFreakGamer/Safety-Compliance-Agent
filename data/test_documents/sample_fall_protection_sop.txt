================================================================================
1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

This Fall Protection Safety Program establishes the requirements and procedures for
protecting employees from fall hazards during construction and maintenance activities
performed by Acme Industrial Services Inc.

This program applies to all employees, subcontractors, and visitors who may be
exposed to fall hazards at any Acme Industrial worksite.

================================================================================
2. REGULATORY REFERENCES
================================================================================

This program is designed to comply with the following regulations:
- OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart M - Fall Protection
- ANSI Z359.1 - Safety Requirements for Personal Fall Arrest Systems

================================================================================
3. DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

Competent Person: An individual who has attended a fall protection awareness course
and has at least 2 years of field experience.

Qualified Person: A person with a recognized degree or professional certificate who
has demonstrated knowledge of fall protection.

Walking/Working Surface: Any horizontal surface on which an employee walks or works.

================================================================================
4. FALL PROTECTION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

4.1 General Requirements

Fall protection is required when employees are working at heights of 10 feet or
more above a lower level. At these heights, employees must be protected by one
of the following methods:

  a) Guardrail systems
  b) Personal fall arrest systems

4.2 Leading Edges

Employees working within 6 feet of a leading edge that is 10 feet or more above
a lower level must use personal fall arrest systems or guardrail systems.

4.3 Hoist Areas

Workers in hoist areas shall be protected by guardrail systems. When guardrails
need to be removed for hoisting operations, work shall proceed with caution.

4.4 Holes and Openings

All floor holes greater than 2 inches in diameter must be covered or guarded.
Covers must be clearly labeled "HOLE" or "COVER" and must be secured to prevent
accidental displacement.

4.5 Ramps and Walkways

Employees on ramps, runways, or walkways with unprotected sides shall use
appropriate fall protection measures when heights exceed the general requirement.

4.6 Roofing Work

Employees engaged in roofing activities on low-slope roofs (4:12 pitch or less)
shall be protected by safety monitoring systems. A designated safety monitor
shall be assigned to watch over workers on the roof.

For steep-slope roofs, employees shall use personal fall arrest systems.

================================================================================
5. FALL PROTECTION SYSTEMS SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

5.1 Guardrail Systems

Guardrail systems shall meet the following specifications:
- Top rail height: 36 inches above the walking/working surface
- Mid-rail installed halfway between the top rail and the surface
- Guardrails must be able to withstand a force of 150 pounds applied
  in any direction
- Smooth surfaced to prevent punctures or lacerations

5.2 Personal Fall Arrest Systems (PFAS)

5.2.1 Body Harnesses and Belts

Employees may use either full body harnesses or body belts when
working at heights. All harnesses must meet ANSI Z359.1 requirements.

5.2.2 Anchorage Points

Anchorage points used for personal fall arrest shall be:
- Capable of supporting at least 3,000 pounds per employee
- Located above the worker's center of gravity when possible

5.2.3 Lanyards and Deceleration Devices

- Maximum lanyard length: 6 feet
- All lanyards must include a deceleration device or be self-retracting
- Snap hooks must be self-locking type

5.2.4 Fall Distance Calculations

Before using personal fall arrest systems, a competent person must verify
that sufficient clearance exists below the worker. Free fall should be
limited to a reasonable distance to prevent contact with lower levels.

5.3 Safety Nets

When safety nets are used as fall protection:
- Nets shall be installed within 15 feet below the work surface
- Nets must extend outward from the work surface at least 8 feet
  regardless of the installation height
- Drop test required after initial installation

================================================================================
6. EQUIPMENT INSPECTION AND MAINTENANCE
================================================================================

6.1 Pre-Use Inspection

All fall protection equipment should be visually inspected before each shift
by the user. Damaged or worn equipment shall be tagged and reported to the
supervisor.

6.2 Periodic Inspection

A competent person shall conduct formal inspections of all fall protection
equipment on a quarterly basis. Inspection records shall be maintained.

6.3 Post-Incident Requirements

Equipment involved in a fall event shall be evaluated by the supervisor to
determine if it can continue to be used. Equipment showing visible damage
should be retired.

================================================================================
7. RESCUE PROCEDURES
================================================================================

In the event of a fall, the site supervisor shall call 911 and wait for
emergency services to arrive. Workers should not attempt self-rescue unless
absolutely necessary. All fall events shall be reported within 24 hours.

================================================================================
8. TRAINING
================================================================================

8.1 Initial Training

All employees who work at heights shall receive fall protection training
before their first work assignment. Training shall cover:
- How to use fall protection equipment
- Hazard recognition
- Emergency procedures

8.2 Training Frequency

Refresher training shall be conducted annually for all employees.

8.3 Training Records

The safety department shall maintain records of all training activities,
including:
- Employee name
- Date of training
- Topics covered

================================================================================
9. SPECIAL ACTIVITIES
================================================================================

9.1 Scaffolding

Workers on scaffolds at heights above the general fall protection threshold
must use appropriate fall protection. Refer to the company scaffolding program
for specific requirements.

9.2 Ladder Safety

Employees using portable ladders must maintain three points of contact at all
times. Fall protection is generally not required when using ladders properly.

================================================================================
10. PROGRAM REVIEW
================================================================================

This Fall Protection Safety Program shall be reviewed annually by the Safety
Department and updated as necessary to reflect changes in regulations,
equipment, or work practices.
